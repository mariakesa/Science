we used chi-squared tests to ask whether two categorical patterns in the mouse’s behavior are related.

The chi-squared test we used is a test of independence.

What conceptual novelty does chi-square open?
1. It lets you ask about structure in categorical worlds

A lot of data is not naturally “average-able.”

Examples:

diagnosis category
vote choice
word type
spike/no-spike
class label
brain region
behavioral state
whether a neuron fired in a bin or not

Here the interesting question is not:
“what is the mean?”

It is:
“is the distribution across categories surprising?”

That is pure chi-square territory.

“does the whole empirical histogram agree with the model?”

Chi-square lives in the land of:

counts
multinomials
tables
occupancy patterns
random assignment
partitions of outcomes

That means it opens questions about:

how finite samples distribute over bins
whether rare categories appear too often or too little
whether observed combinatorial patterns are too organized to be chance
